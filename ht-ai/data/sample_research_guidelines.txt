Research Guidelines and Procedures

INTRODUCTION
This document outlines the research procedures and guidelines for all students and faculty conducting research at our university.

RESEARCH ETHICS

Institutional Review Board (IRB)
All research involving human subjects must be reviewed and approved by the Institutional Review Board.

IRB Requirements:
- Complete IRB application form
- Submit informed consent forms
- Provide recruitment materials
- Include data security plan
- Allow 4-6 weeks for review process

Exempt Research Categories:
- Educational research in established settings
- Anonymous surveys with minimal risk
- Existing data analysis (publicly available)
- Program evaluation/quality improvement

Plagiarism and Academic Integrity
Researchers must maintain highest standards of academic integrity:

- Proper citation of all sources
- No self-plagiarism
- Data accuracy and transparency
- Authorship criteria must be met

DATA MANAGEMENT

Data Collection Procedures
Standardized data collection protocols:

1. Pre-research planning
   - Define research questions
   - Select appropriate methodology
   - Prepare data collection instruments
   - Train research assistants

2. Data collection
   - Use standardized forms
   - Maintain consistent procedures
   - Monitor data quality
   - Address issues immediately

3. Data storage
   - Secure encrypted storage
   - Regular backup procedures
   - Access control protocols
   - Retention schedule compliance

Data Privacy and Confidentiality
Protect participant privacy:

- De-identify all personal data
- Use participant codes instead of names
- Store consent forms separately
- Limit data access to research team
- Follow GDPR/FERPA regulations

PUBLICATION AND DISSEMINATION

Authorship Guidelines
Authorship criteria (ICMJE standards):
1. Substantial contribution to conception/design
2. Drafting or critical revision of work
3. Final approval of version
4. Accountability for all aspects

Contributors not meeting all criteria should be acknowledged.

Publication Process
Standard publication workflow:

1. Internal review by research team
2. Department chair review
3. IRB compliance verification
4. Statistical review (if applicable)
5. External peer review
6. Publication decision

Open Access Policy
University supports open access publishing:

- Institutional repository deposit required
- Creative Commons licensing encouraged
- Publication funds available
- Funder compliance monitoring

FUNDING AND GRANTS

Grant Application Process
Standard grant application timeline:

- 3 months before deadline: Initial proposal
- 2 months before: Budget development
- 1 month before: Internal review
- Submit: External application
- 1-3 months: Review period

Budget Categories
Allowable expenses:
- Personnel salaries and benefits
- Equipment purchases
- Supplies and materials
- Travel expenses
- Publication costs
- Indirect costs (overhead)

Unallowable expenses:
- Entertainment costs
- Personal expenses
- Capital improvements
- Lobbying activities

COMPLIANCE AND MONITORING

Research Compliance
Required compliance training:

- Human subjects protection (CITI)
- Responsible conduct of research
- Data security training
- Conflict of interest disclosure

Annual compliance reviews:
- IRB protocol renewals
- Data security audits
- Financial compliance checks
- Training record updates

Research Misconduct
Definition: Fabrication, falsification, or plagiarism in research.

Reporting procedures:
1. Report to Research Integrity Officer
2. Preliminary assessment (10 days)
3. Formal investigation (if needed)
4. Committee review and recommendations
5. Final determination and sanctions

RESOURCES AND SUPPORT

Research Support Services
Available resources:

- Grant writing assistance
- Library research services

- Publication assistance
